# Security & Code of Conduct

## Account & Password Security

Use a unique, company-issued password manager entry for every work account — never reuse a personal password for a work system. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory on all systems that support it. Report a lost or stolen device to IT Security immediately; do not wait until you're back at a computer to file the report.

## Approved Software

New software must be requested and approved through the IT Portal before installation on a company device. Approval considers licensing cost and data-handling risk. IT maintains a list of pre-approved software in the IT Portal that does not require a new request.

## Company Code & Intellectual Property

Company source code, credentials, and internal documentation may not be copied to personal accounts, personal devices, or third-party services outside of company-approved tools. All code, designs, and inventions created as part of your work belong to the company, per your employment agreement.

## Gifts & Conflicts of Interest

Employees may not accept gifts over $75 in value from a vendor, client, or partner without disclosing it to their manager and Legal. This mirrors the rule on gifts *given* to clients described in the Expense Reimbursement policy — the goal in both directions is to avoid even the appearance of a conflict of interest. Disclose any outside financial interest that could reasonably influence a business decision you're involved in.
