# Paid Time Off & Leave

## Vacation & PTO Accrual

Full-time employees accrue 1.67 days of PTO per month (20 days/year), starting from their first day of employment. PTO accrual is prorated for part-time employees based on scheduled hours. Unused PTO carries over up to a maximum of 5 days into the next calendar year; anything above that is forfeited on January 1st unless local law requires otherwise.

## Requesting Time Off

Submit time-off requests through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance for anything longer than 2 days. Your manager must approve the request before it's considered confirmed — a pending request does not guarantee the time off. For same-day or emergency absences, notify your manager directly by message or call as early as possible and log the absence in the HR portal afterward.

## Sick Leave

Sick leave is separate from PTO and does not require advance notice. Notify your manager before your normal start time if you're unable to work. No documentation is required for absences of 2 days or fewer; a doctor's note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

## Parental & Bereavement Leave

Parental leave is 12 weeks paid for the primary caregiver and 4 weeks paid for the secondary caregiver, available to employees who have completed 6 months of service. Bereavement leave is 5 paid days for immediate family and 2 paid days for extended family, and does not need to be taken consecutively.

## Jury Duty Leave

Jury duty is fully paid for up to 10 business days per calendar year. Provide your summons to HR as soon as you receive it so your absence can be recorded correctly.
